Concrete Roads

Concrete roads are the fastest variety of road that can be constructed, offering a 30% movement speed bonus when properly constructed. However, road builders must be diligent when constructing concrete roads to prevent them from cracking or being trodden on while wet, as this will remove the speed bonus.

Concrete Road

The first step in making a concrete road is mixing the liquid concrete. Use a Mixer to combine water with any two stone dusts, one of either calcite or marble dust, and gypsum dust, or any three stone dusts with clay dust to make Liquid Concrete.

Then add the liquid concrete in a Mixer set to Programmed Circuit #7 with more water to dilute it into Wet Concrete Mix.

Once Wet Concrete has been mixed, it can be picked up in a bucket and placed on a Base Course Block where it will spread to adjacent blocks. The concrete will harden after one day, and until it hardens care must be taken that no creatures walk through the wet concrete as this will create an uneven surface that provides no speed bonus. Footprints can be smoothed out with a Mattock.

Pouring a Concrete Road

Shrinkage cracking is also a concern as concrete sets. If concrete is poured in areas more than four blocks wide, blocks that are farther than two blocks from the nearest Edge, Control Joint, or Textured Finish will crack when set. This can be prevented by pouring concrete in smaller areas, using a Mattock to add Control Joints, or by applying a Textured Finish.

Control Joints

Textured Finishes are created by pressing Bricks, Flagstones, or Smooth Stone Blocks into drying concrete to create an architectural finish. These blocks also act as Control Joints for preventing cracking. Once concrete has finished drying, a Chisel can be used to form slabs and stairs.
